How EquiCurve Works

Dynamic Demand Model

Our model captures real-world crowdfunding dynamics with multiplicative effects:

D(t) = α × E(t)^β × P(t)^(-γ) × ε(t)

Incentive Alignment

Model the interaction between platform (principal) and entrepreneur (agent) to optimize outcomes for both parties.

Real-time Results

See instant visualization of how price, effort, and platform strategies affect your campaign success.

Understanding the Parameters

Demand Function Components

  • α: Base demand level (market size)
  • E(t): Entrepreneur effort (0-10 scale)
  • β: Effort elasticity (typically 0.3-0.7)
  • P(t): Token price at time t
  • γ: Price elasticity (typically 0.8-1.5)
  • ε(t): Random market noise

Campaign Dynamics

  • Time Pressure: Demand increases as deadline approaches
  • Network Effects: Success attracts more backers
  • Platform Boost: Marketing multiplier effect
  • Threshold: All-or-nothing funding model

Academic Foundation

This project bridges economic theory with Web3 innovation, applying principal-agent models to decentralized crowdfunding mechanisms.

Key Research Areas

  • • Dynamic pricing mechanisms in token sales
  • • Information asymmetry and signaling in Web3
  • • Optimal incentive alignment between platforms and entrepreneurs
  • • Network effects and momentum in crowdfunding
  • • Bonding curves and automated market makers

Based on research from: Belleflamme et al. (2014), Mollick (2014), Chemla & Tinn (2020), and recent Web3 tokenomics literature.
